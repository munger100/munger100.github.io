<h2>The incenter of a triangle</h2><div id="problem_info" class="info"><h3>Problem 482</h3><span>Published on Sunday, 28th September 2014, 07:00 am; Solved by 122; Difficulty rating: 85%</span></div>
<div class="problem_content" role="problem">
<p>
ABC is an integer sided triangle with incenter I and perimeter p.<br />
The segments IA, IB and IC have integral length as well. 
</p>
<p>
Let L = p + |IA| + |IB| + |IC|. 
</p>
<p>
Let S(P) = ∑L for all such triangles where p ≤ P. For example, S(10<sup>3</sup>) = 3619.
</p>
<p>
Find S(10<sup>7</sup>).
</p>
